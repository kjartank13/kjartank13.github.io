Brynja is a musician from Iceland. She was surrounded by music during her upbringing but it wasn’t until a brief period working with a street theater group in Norway in 2011 that she discovered a passion for writing and singing her own songs. Un-phased by knowing only a handful of guitar chords, she started pouring out songs and lyrics that captured the feelings she felt at the time. Since then she has continued writing and performing as well as releasing an EP with simple but emotionally powerful songs. 

Her latest release is the song Liar where she takes a song she created during her early writing and explores a new sound and a new feeling that the song now has to her. During the recording she started to get more into the production side of music and has since started studying Audio Engineering in Amsterdam.

The song came to her in the car on the way to her grandparents summer house. On her arrival she picked up the guitar and tried to play the song but was forced to adjust the melody to the limited amount of guitar chords she knew at the time. The lyrics describe her inner dialogue about whether she should lie to herself and others about her feelings, maybe that could help her feel better.

In 2017 Brynja felt it was time to dust off the old song and record it properly. She wanted to take the feeling of the song into a different direction than she had before, so she sought production help from musician Baldur Hjörleifsson. Together in a Berlin apartment, they layed the foundation of the songs new fresh sound. Many other friends and musicians lent their talents to the creation of Liar and Davíð Hólm Júlíusson took care of the final mixing.

The inspiration for the video's visuals came to Brynja on a sleepless night. They came to her fully formed and she went straight to work to make it a reality. 

The video was shot in a single take on 16mm film just before sunset on an autumn night in Iceland. Brynja is joined by a duo of female dancers and all three are clad in denim-on-denim as they tear into a dance routine they choreographed for the video. As daylight was fading fast, they knew this was their only chance to get it right. The energy in the air felt magical and the resulting video clearly captures the powerful moment.
